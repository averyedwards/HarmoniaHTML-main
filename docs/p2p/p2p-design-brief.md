## Section 1: Existing Design System Audit

### The Visual Language of the Harmonia Site

The existing pages compose a coherent visual argument through a small number of principles applied with uncommon consistency: warmth before modernity, restraint before elaboration, and the animation as a vehicle for meaning rather than decoration. To understand this is not to catalogue the CSS variables, it is to grasp why the page feels the way it does when you first load it, before you have read a single word.

The palette operates through a hierarchy of warmth. The cream at `#FAF6F1` is not merely a background; it is the membrane through which all other colours are perceived. Against it, the gold at `#D4A853` carries the weight of the primary accent: it marks what matters, it highlights where Harmonia appears in the comparison columns, it draws the eye along the note-box border, and it glows in the dark theme at `#F0C86E` with a luminosity calibrated precisely to the shift from the warm black of `#12090A`. The maroon at `#722F37` is the secondary accent, the colour of the card titles, the comparison column labels, the nav link hover state; it speaks of something older and more serious than the gold, something closer to the institution than the startup. These two colours never fight. The gold is applied at accent weight; the maroon is applied at structural weight; the cream, the blush, and the rose absorb both without registering the tension. In dark mode, the palette does not invert, it warms: the card surface becomes `#2D1A1C`, which is neither black nor brown but something between wine and shadow, and every element that was cream becomes a deep burgundy-black that still reads as warm.

The typography functions as a system of register, not merely hierarchy. Cormorant Garamond carries the section titles, the card titles, the large Cormorant numerals of the stat cards (rendered at `3.5rem`, `line-height: 1`), and the footer logo text. It signals: this is where the claim is made, this is the thing that must be believed. DM Sans carries everything else: the body, the comparison column text, the button labels, the stat card labels in their uppercase `letter-spacing: 0.05em` treatment, the note-box heading in its gold uppercase. The combination creates a tonal register that is simultaneously scholarly and accessible, the kind of typography that a serious institution might use to address an intelligent public.

The navigation structure is fixed and frosted, sitting at `backdrop-filter: blur(20px)` over a background that is 92% opaque, with a bottom border that in dark mode acquires a gold glow shadow (`box-shadow: 0 4px 20px rgba(240, 200, 110, 0.08)`) that is almost imperceptible but is precisely the detail that makes the dark-mode nav feel composed rather than merely inverted. The hamburger menu, when opened, becomes a full-viewport overlay centred on Cormorant Garamond links at `1.6rem`, each one stagger-animated with `fadeInUp` at fifty-millisecond intervals, seven links, seven beats, a cadence that makes the act of opening the mobile menu feel like the presentation of something. Every page carries identical nav and identical footer: the footer in maroon-deep `#5C1A1B`, the Cormorant logo text in gold-champagne at `1.3rem`, the footer links in `rgba(245, 217, 138, 0.7)`, the copyright in `rgba(255, 255, 255, 0.5)`. The page entrance animation, a `pageFadeIn` keyframe that begins at `opacity: 0` and `translateY(15px)`, completes in 0.5 seconds and is preceded by a `520ms` overflow lock to prevent the vertical shift from triggering a momentary scrollbar. All of this is managed by `shared.js` and the page system requires no modification to accommodate a new page: the new page simply carries the `page active` class and defers to the same shared stylesheet.

The stat-card component achieves its effect through scale contrast: a Cormorant Garamond number at `3.5rem` in maroon or gold, set above an uppercase DM Sans label at `0.9rem`, `letter-spacing: 0.05em`, and below both a citation link at `0.65rem` opacity `0.6` that becomes `color: var(--gold)` on hover. The card has no border, no shadow, no background; it is pure typographic composition. The three cards sit in a CSS grid of `repeat(3, 1fr)` at a maximum width of 900px, centred, with a `border-bottom: 1px solid rgba(212, 168, 83, 0.2)` below the row that marks the transition from data to prose.

The philosophy-block that follows the stat cards is a centred column at `max-width: 650px` carrying a lead-text at `1.25rem`, `line-height: 1.6`, in `var(--maroon-deep)` in light mode and `var(--rose)` in dark. It is the site's primary voice channel: direct, declarative, built on short sentences that land with authority. The component is not a box; there is no border, no padding, no background. It is simply text given room.

The science slider is the most compositionally complex element in the existing site: a full-viewport-width horizontal scroll container, breaking out of the `max-width: 1200px` container via negative margin calculus (`margin-left: calc(-50vw + 50%)`), holding four `science-card` panels at `min(900px, 85vw)` flex width, each with `scroll-snap-align: center` and a bottom padding dot-pagination row. Each card is a `flex-direction: column` container with a `card-header` row at the top (a 100px square icon placeholder on the left, a `card-title-group` on the right with a Cormorant Garamond title at `2.5rem` in maroon and a subtitle at `1rem`), and a `card-content` grid of three equal columns at the bottom, one per comparison label. The gold border on hover (`border-color: var(--gold)`) and the box-shadow lift (`0 15px 50px rgba(212, 168, 83, 0.12)`) complete the interactive state. The background is `rgba(114, 47, 55, 0.04)` in light mode and `rgba(255, 255, 255, 0.03)` in dark: barely-there tints that tell you the card is a surface without announcing it.

What makes the science slider singular is the animation system inside each card. Every icon begins at center-card and journeys to the 100px icon slot in the top-left corner as its content reveals. The eye on the Visual card starts as a closed arc, the upper lid path `M30 50 Q50 35 70 50` and lower lid path `M35 50 Q50 65 65 50` sitting together; as the card enters its animated state, the lids part along the y-axis, the pupil (a gold vertical line at `x=50`) fades in, and the background diamond at 45-degree rotation materialises at `opacity: 0.2`. The personality triangle begins with three maroon dots at vertices (`cx=22,cy=27`; `cx=78,cy=27`; `cx=50,cy=85`), the gold inner lines drawing themselves via `stroke-dashoffset` from `40` to `0`, then the golden center circle popping in at `scale(0)` to `scale(1)` via a spring cubic-bezier, and finally the three dashed outer arcs revealed by a mask path animating its own `stroke-dashoffset` from `120` to `0`. The DNA helix on the genetic card begins with both curves hidden (`stroke-dashoffset: 200`), then draws them simultaneously as the icon container rotates `540deg` on the Y axis (one and a half full turns), amber particles tracing the helix paths during the draw, electric crackle bolts flashing on the rungs, an energy wave expanding from center to invisibility, and a final gold drop-shadow glow that settles into the header icon position. The synthesis card animates three miniature icons from triangle positions toward the card center where they collide, producing an impact flash, a radial gold-to-transparent particle spray in nine directions, a fog of `radial-gradient` gold that expands and fades, and then the Harmonia knot drawing itself at center before spinning `180deg` and travelling on CSS transitions to the header corner. The knot receives its gold-to-maroon gradient from a `linearGradient` SVG def injected into the body by `shared.js`, which also listens for theme changes via `MutationObserver` and reapplies the correct gradient on every toggle.

The note-box component is structurally elemental: a left border of `2px solid var(--gold)`, a background of `linear-gradient(90deg, rgba(212, 168, 83, 0.08), transparent)`, a heading in uppercase DM Sans gold at `0.9rem`, and body text in maroon-deep at `1rem`, `line-height: 1.6`. Its function is to carry supplemental context without creating a visual break in the page flow; the gradient dissolves toward the right, ensuring the box opens into the surrounding cream rather than closing against it.

The `fade-in` animation class produces a `0.6s ease` transition from `opacity: 0; transform: translateY(20px)` to the resolved state, with `.delay-1` through `.delay-4` at 100ms intervals. These classes are applied at page-load level in `why-harmonia.html` to the stat cards and the philosophy block; scroll-triggered fade-ins for deeper sections are handled in `app.js` via IntersectionObserver, which adds an `animated` class at the moment each element crosses the viewport threshold.

**What transfers to p2p.html, what does not, what requires adaptation.** The nav, footer, CSS variables, typography system, button styles, the `fade-in` and `delay-*` animation classes, the `pageFadeIn` entrance, the `note-box` component, the `philosophy-block` structure, and the `stat-card` structure all transfer directly and must be replicated exactly. The horizontal science slider is specific to the why-harmonia multi-card comparison use case and does not transfer; p2p requires a different container for its phase cards. The radar SVG on the index hero transfers as a potential Phase 2 Resonance illustration but requires adaptation. The synthesis knot gradient injection system in `shared.js` transfers and must be invoked on any page that uses SVG path fill animations. The comparison column three-column grid is specific to the Science/Apps/Harmonia argument and does not transfer. The eye, personality, DNA, and synthesis card animations are specific to why-harmonia, but the vocabulary they establish, thin gold strokes, maroon accent dots, stroke-dashoffset draw animations, geometric forms assembling from constituent parts, is the vocabulary that the new p2p.html icons must speak.

## Section 2: Design Reference Synthesis

### Search 1: Scientific Study Recruitment Landing Page Design

The strongest pattern that emerged across this research domain is what Nielsen Norman Group calls the commitment hierarchy: people arrive at an unfamiliar page at the lowest level of engagement, and any attempt to extract high-commitment information before the page has earned the right to ask for it will fail, not because the user is uninterested, but because the ask arrived before the trust. For the p2p page, this principle has direct structural consequences. The email capture in the hero must not ask for name, age, location, or anything beyond email; it must frame the ask as an act of interest rather than an act of registration, and it must do so on a page that has already established, through restraint, through the weight of its typography, through the precision of its statistical claims, that this is a legitimate scientific undertaking and not a startup hoping to harvest leads. The corollary pattern is cognitive fluency: users process the page emotionally before they process it rationally, and a page whose design is uncluttered, whose typographic hierarchy is immediately legible, whose tone is consistent and serious, will have already begun to earn trust before the first sentence has been read. The adaptation for Harmonia is to carry the existing site's warm restraint into the p2p context without allowing it to feel austere: the cream and gold palette must do the emotional work that the institution does in a university recruitment page, signalling care, precision, and welcome without the clinical coldness that afflicts pages from actual research institutions. One standing-out element at a time is the operating rule. The hero's primary conversion action is the email capture; nothing in the hero competes with it.

### Search 2: Phase Timeline Journey UI Design

The most transferable pattern from this research is the vertical or horizontal stepper with numbered phases and a visual connector that signals both sequence and dependency: each stage visibly attaches to the one before it, so the user understands not just that there are three phases, but that Phase 2 becomes possible because Phase 1 was completed, and Phase 3 becomes possible because both prior phases have run. The endowed progress effect, whereby users who feel they have already begun a journey are more likely to complete it, suggests that the phase overview on p2p should frame each user who reads the page as someone already in motion, already at the threshold of Phase 1, rather than a bystander contemplating an unfamiliar commitment. The most effective timeline designs in the research material use numbered roundels or filled circles as the connective tissue between phase cards, with a colour or weight change to indicate progression, and a short connecting line or stroke between each roundel and the next. The specific adaptation for Harmonia: the three phase cards will be displayed in a vertical stack on mobile and a horizontal three-column grid at desktop, with a gold roundel bearing the phase number positioned above each card, connected by a thin gold horizontal line on desktop and a gold vertical stroke on mobile. The Calibration roundel (Phase 1) is filled with the maroon; the Resonance roundel (Phase 2) is outlined, gold border on cream; the Chemistry roundel (Phase 3) is outlined in gold with a subtle pulsing opacity animation, signalling that it is the destination toward which the journey is building. The line connecting roundels is `rgba(212, 168, 83, 0.3)`, a presence rather than a declaration, because the argument should be made by the card content, not by the connective geometry.

### Search 3: Venn Diagram CSS SVG Animation

The core mechanism for the coalescing Venn animation in Phase 2 (Resonance) is established by the CodePen pattern of `mix-blend-mode: multiply` combined with `transform: translate()` keyframes: two circles, each with a semi-transparent fill in different tones, begin in a separated state and animate toward a central overlap via `ease-in-out` keyframes, the intersection zone revealing a third colour that is the chromatic product of both. For Harmonia, both circles begin in maroon at approximately `rgba(114, 47, 55, 0.4)`, so that their intersection produces a deeper, saturated maroon-gold, and the shared personality trait text, "You both prefer harmony over confrontation," fades in only within the overlap zone. The SVG approach is preferable to the CSS pure approach because it allows the circle paths to be given `stroke` in gold without requiring the `border-radius` trick, and because it integrates cleanly with the existing SVG vocabulary of the site. The pure CSS approach with `mix-blend-mode` is adequate for the static illustration in Phase 1, where no motion is required and the Venn is simply a concept diagram. The animated coalescing version in Phase 2 requires JavaScript to orchestrate the three stages: initial separation, the movement inward over `1.2s cubic-bezier(0.4, 0, 0.2, 1)`, and the text reveal in the overlap zone via opacity transition at `0.4s ease` beginning at the midpoint of the approach. The circles do not use GSAP; they use CSS transitions triggered by a class addition on scroll-entry, consistent with how the science card animations are managed. The timing is unhurried because the coalescing of two personalities is not a collision but a recognition.

### Search 4: Gamified Progress Indicator Web Design

The tension this research clarifies is between the gamification patterns appropriate to a consumer app context (Duolingo streaks, LinkedIn profile completion bars) and the seriousness that a TRL 9 scientific validation demands. The resolution is to borrow the psychological mechanism, the endowed progress effect, the sense of forward momentum, without borrowing the aesthetic register of the game. What this means practically: the phase overview section communicates that Phase 1 is open now, and the act of registering an email is not the beginning of an onboarding flow but the first step of a scientific contribution whose outputs the user will personally receive, which is an entirely different kind of progress. No progress bar with a percentage. No gamification language. Instead, the three phase cards carry inside each one a brief statement of what the user will have completed and what they will have received when that phase ends: after Phase 1, a visual preference profile and a personality radar chart; after Phase 2, a personalised match insights report and a cross-phase comparison; after Phase 3 if eligible, a full tripartite report. This is the endowed progress effect applied without any of its usual UI ornamentation: the user imagines forward into the completed state, and that imagining is what converts.

### Search 5: Waitlist Referral Share UI Pattern

The clearest lesson from this research is that the share mechanism converts when it frames the act of sharing as a benefit to the person doing the sharing, not a service to the platform requesting it. For Harmonia, the Phase 3 Chemistry section is London-only, and the expansion to other cities depends on critical mass; this creates a natural, authentic scarcity that does not require manufactured urgency. The share section should be framed as: your city could be next, and the way it becomes next is through collective participation; here is a link to share with people who should be part of this. The UI for this is a single copy-link button, gold-bordered on cream, that produces a toast notification confirming the link has been copied, without any social-platform icons, because the Harmonia register does not include the visual vocabulary of social media shares and including those icons would dilute the institutional tone. The button sits inside a compact, contained card: dark maroon-deep background in both modes, a headline in Cormorant Garamond gold, a single sentence of supporting text in DM Sans rose, and the copy-link button centred below. On copy, the button label changes from "Copy link" to "Copied" for `2000ms` before reverting. There is no position counter, no leaderboard, no referral score: those patterns belong to consumer conversion funnels, and this page is a recruitment instrument for a scientific study.

### Search 6: Expandable Read More Section Elegant Web Design

The research is definitive that the accordion pattern works when the header text is descriptive enough that the user can choose with genuine information which sections to expand, and fails when the header is too vague to distinguish the hidden content from the adjacent content. For the Harmonia "read more" expandables, the trigger text must name what lies beneath it precisely: not "Read more" but "The methodology: how we measure perceived similarity" or "The evidence: immunogenetics and HLA compatibility research." The collapsed state shows the trigger text, a right-aligned chevron in gold at `0.75rem`, pointing down; on expand, the chevron rotates `180deg` via `transition: transform 0.3s ease`, and the panel height transitions from `0` to `auto` via a JavaScript-measured `max-height` technique (setting `max-height` to `0` initially and to the `scrollHeight` value on expand, with `overflow: hidden` and `transition: max-height 0.4s ease-in-out`). The expanded content sits inside the same warm card surface as the section it extends, with a `border-top: 1px solid rgba(212, 168, 83, 0.15)` separating it from the summary above, and a slightly reduced font-size of `0.95rem` to signal that this is the extended, detailed register rather than the primary voice. The design does not use a full accordion with only one panel open at a time; multiple read-more sections can be open simultaneously because the user may wish to read the methodology for Phase 1 while also reviewing the Phase 3 genetics research, and forcing a single-open constraint would interrupt that reading flow. The paragraph weight inside the expanded section is `400`, the line-height is `1.7`, and the maximum column width is `65ch` to preserve comfortable reading measure.

### Specific Decisions

**The three-phase timeline visual.** A horizontal three-column grid at desktop (`repeat(3, 1fr)` at `gap: 2rem`) and a vertical single-column stack on mobile, with the column order Calibration / Resonance / Chemistry reading left to right. Above the card grid, a connector row: three gold roundels (24px diameter, `border-radius: 50%`) numbered 1, 2, 3 in Cormorant Garamond, connected by a thin `1px` gold line at `rgba(212, 168, 83, 0.25)`. Phase 1 roundel: filled `var(--maroon)`, numeral in white. Phase 2 roundel: `border: 1px solid var(--gold)`, numeral in gold, cream fill. Phase 3 roundel: `border: 1px solid var(--gold)`, numeral in gold at `opacity: 0.6`, cream fill, with a `pulse-ring` keyframe at `opacity: 0.3` radiating outward to signal that this phase is building. On mobile, the connector is a vertical gold line at `width: 1px` between each roundel, and the roundels are centred on the left edge of each card.

**How the read-more expandables will behave.** Height transition via JavaScript `scrollHeight` technique, `0.4s ease-in-out`. Trigger: a row containing the trigger label in `0.85rem DM Sans font-weight: 500 var(--gold)` and a `chevron-right` SVG icon at `0.75rem` rotated `90deg` pointing downward, transitioning to `270deg` pointing upward on expand. The row sits below the phase section's summary prose, within the full Phase 1, Phase 2, and Phase 3 sections of the page (Sections 3, 4, and 5), at the base of each section's descriptive content, with `padding-top: 1rem` and a gold-tinted top border. This expandable mechanism belongs exclusively to the full phase sections; it does not exist on the Section 2 overview cards, which are orientation devices only and carry no expandable trigger of any kind. No expand-all control. Each full phase section has its own independent expandable.

**The Venn diagram animation approach.** Two SVG circles, each `r=80` in a `240x120` viewBox, initially centred at `cx=60` and `cx=180` respectively, in `rgba(114, 47, 55, 0.35)` fill with `stroke: var(--gold)` at `1px`. On scroll-entry, a class addition triggers CSS `left: 120` transitions on both circles over `1.2s cubic-bezier(0.4, 0, 0.2, 1)` toward `cx=100` and `cx=140`, creating a 60-unit overlap. In the overlap zone, a `clipPath` reveals a third layer: gold fill at `opacity: 0.15` and the shared-trait text at `0.8rem DM Sans` in maroon, fading in at `0.4s ease` after a `0.8s` delay. Pure CSS transitions, no GSAP, consistent with the existing animation system.

**How the email capture field will be styled.** A single-row flex container at `max-width: 480px` centred below the hero prose: an `input[type=email]` at `flex: 1` with `background: var(--card-bg)`, `border: 1px solid rgba(212, 168, 83, 0.3)`, `border-radius: 4px 0 0 4px`, `padding: 0.85rem 1.2rem`, `font-size: 1rem`, `color: var(--navy)`, and `placeholder: "your@email.com"` in `var(--slate)`; immediately adjacent, a `btn btn-primary` at `border-radius: 0 4px 4px 0`, `white-space: nowrap`, label "Notify Me". Above the input row, a single line of DM Sans text at `0.85rem` in `var(--slate)`, centred, reading "Testing pool opens early April"; this line carries no countdown, no urgency mechanic, no date commitment beyond the season, and its restraint is the point. On mobile below 480px, the row breaks to column: input full-width, button full-width beneath it. The overall container has `margin-top: 2rem` below the hero lead-text.

**How the share mechanic will be implemented.** A contained card using the existing science-card visual treatment (`background: rgba(114, 47, 55, 0.04)`, `border: 1px solid rgba(212, 168, 83, 0.25)`, `border-radius: 12px`, `padding: 2rem`) at `max-width: 600px` centred. Inside: a Cormorant Garamond heading at `1.8rem` in maroon, a single-sentence DM Sans body line in slate, and a `btn btn-secondary` centred with label "Copy link", `min-width: 180px`. On click, the `navigator.clipboard.writeText()` call writes the page URL, the button label transitions to "Copied" in `var(--maroon)` for `2000ms`, and a toast element (`position: fixed; bottom: 2rem; left: 50%; transform: translateX(-50%)`) appears for the same duration with `"Link copied"` in DM Sans at `0.9rem` against a maroon-deep background. The toast uses `pageFadeIn` animation at `0.3s` forward and `0.3s` reverse before removal.

## Section 3: Section-by-Section Layout Proposal

**Section 1: Hero.** Full-viewport height minimum (`min-height: calc(100vh - 80px)`) with a single centred column at `max-width: 780px`, matching the centred why-harmonia layout rather than the two-column index.html grid, because the hero's primary task is philosophical declaration rather than product demonstration and a centred single column creates the necessary gravity. The section background carries the same radial gradient float animation as the index hero (`rgba(212, 168, 83, 0.06)` at 30% and `rgba(139, 58, 58, 0.05)` at 70%), subtly warm and in slow motion. The Cormorant Garamond section title at `clamp(2.2rem, 4.5vw, 3.8rem)` appears first with the `fade-in` class; below it, a `philosophy-block` lead-text paragraph carrying the page's named philosophical thesis: "All Desire is a Desire for Being," Harmonia's own position on the problem, attributed to no external thinker, belonging to the argument itself as its foundation. The prose beneath the thesis must articulate, with precision, the distinction between desire and want, because that distinction is the architectural principle from which the entire page argument is constructed: every compatibility platform ever built has optimised for what people desire, the dopamine, the scroll, the novelty, the infinite parade of faces, the algorithmic delivery of whatever triggers engagement and extends the session; none has optimised for what people want, which is to be known, to be understood, to have the invisible signals of attraction and compatibility made explicit so that two people can recognise each other without the platform standing between them; Harmonia is the engine that makes the implicit explicit, that takes the signals buried in language and in the eye and in biology and translates them into a form that two people can use. This prose carries no startup register; it is declarative and serious, built from short sentences that accumulate into a position. Immediately below the desire-versus-want argument, before the email capture row, a second structural element is required and must be treated as architectural rather than ornamental: the statement "We use AI as a translator, never as a determinant," which defines Harmonia's relationship to its own technology and must appear on the page in its own typographic register, legible and unambiguous, so that the user understands from the first screen that the platform does not decide for them; the AI reads signals the user cannot articulate, surfaces patterns buried in language, in the eye, in biology, and presents those patterns to the user so that the user, and only the user, can decide; the user is the decider and the system is the translator, and this is not a tagline but an architectural principle whose presence in the hero is non-negotiable. Below these two prose elements, the email capture row, carrying "Testing pool opens early April" above the input, `placeholder: "your@email.com"`, and a "Notify Me" button. No stat cards in this section: the hero builds on thesis, not data. The section transitions into Section 2 via a natural scroll: the hero's bottom has `padding-bottom: 5rem`, and Section 2 begins at the viewport threshold with a background shift from cream to blush.

**Section 2: How It Works (Phase Overview).** A `max-width: 1100px` centred container with a section title in Cormorant Garamond, then the connector row (roundels and gold line), then the three-column phase card grid below. Each phase card uses the `science-card` visual treatment with its gold border-on-hover, carrying inside it: the phase icon (100px SVG, top-left), the phase name in Cormorant Garamond at `1.8rem` maroon, and a two-sentence summary in DM Sans at `1rem`. Nothing else. No expandable trigger, no chevron, no read-more row of any kind appears on these overview cards; the overview is a map of the journey, an orientation device whose function is to tell the user that three phases exist and that they escalate, and its restraint in carrying only the icon, the name, and the two-sentence summary is precisely what gives it that orientation function without overwhelming the user before they have reached the sections where each phase is given its full argument. The "read more" expandables exist exclusively in Sections 3, 4, and 5 of the page, where Calibration, Resonance, and Chemistry are each given the space to unfold their methodology; the overview is the summary of what the journey contains, and the full phase sections are the journey itself. Scroll-triggered fade-in fires separately on the section title (first), then on the connector row (`delay-1`), then on each card in left-to-right sequence (`delay-1`, `delay-2`, `delay-3`). The section background is `var(--blush)`, the lightest differentiation from the cream hero, creating a temperature shift without a hard boundary. The section transitions into Section 3 via padding and a return to the `var(--cream)` background.

**Section 3: Phase 1, Calibration.** A two-column layout at desktop, `1.2fr 1fr`: left column holding the descriptive prose, the personality question summary, and the read-more expandable; right column holding the static Venn diagram illustration and the 1-to-5 face rating scale visual. On tablet and below, the layout collapses to a single column with the visual elements stacking below the prose. The Venn illustration is a static SVG, centred in the right column, with no animation; it is a concept diagram, and its stillness is deliberate, the calm of a claim made without performance. The rating scale visual is a horizontal row of five numbered nodes in gold circles connected by a thin gold line, labels at `0.7rem` below each node: 1 = Not my type, 3 = Uncertain, 5 = Very attractive; it functions as a simple communication of the rating mechanic, not an interactive element. Scroll-triggered fade-ins fire first on the prose column, then on the visual column with `delay-2`. The read-more expandable, when open, extends the left column below the main prose with methodology text covering six distinct dimensions of the Phase 1 system, each of which must be described in layman-accessible prose compelling enough to earn the user's time. The first dimension is the Seven Deadly Sins framework: how the six personality questions map onto seven behavioural dimensions, why those dimensions were chosen, and what it means to sit at various positions along each axis from virtue to vice. The second dimension is the perceived similarity principle: what the research demonstrates about how perceived similarity, the sense that another person is like you, predicts initial attraction far more powerfully than actual measured trait similarity, and how the Phase 1 personality assessment is the instrument through which each user's position in that dimensional space is established so that perceived similarity can be calculated during Phase 2. The third dimension is lie detection and inconsistency detection: the AI does not merely read what a user writes; it detects inconsistencies between what they claim and how they write, between stated values and revealed behavioural signals in the language itself; if a user claims they never grow frustrated but their word choices, sentence structures, and tonal patterns reveal impatience or confrontation, the system registers the discrepancy; the personality profile that emerges reflects who the user actually is, not the self-image they wish to project, and this distinction is the source of the system's accuracy. The fourth dimension is response style detection: if a user rushes through the questionnaire pushing every dimension to its maximum (extreme answers across every question, fast completion time), the system detects that pattern and classifies it as a response style artifact rather than a genuine personality signal; if a user provides disengaged, flat, midpoint answers to everything, especially combined with short word counts and rapid completion, the system detects that too; quality is measured, not assumed, and the system's output is only as accurate as the user's engagement with it. The fifth dimension is personality clustering: after processing the six responses, the system maps the user into a personality cluster, grouping them with other users whose behavioural patterns across the seven dimensions are similar; this clustering is the mechanism that powers Phase 2, determining who appears in a user's selection pool and how the perceived similarity score between any two users is calculated. The sixth dimension is the visual neural network, and it is the centrepiece of the Phase 1 methodology explanation: the two-stage architecture that makes the visual system fundamentally different from anything any other platform uses. In the first stage, the model learns what most people find attractive; it studies how large numbers of people rate the same faces and learns the patterns that emerge, the features and proportions and qualities that produce consistent aesthetic agreement across the population; this is the foundation, an understanding of aesthetic commonality at scale. In the second stage, the model freezes that baseline understanding and turns its attention to the individual user: it takes their specific ratings, the faces scored high and the faces scored low, and it learns how their preferences deviate from the consensus; where does this user diverge from what most people find attractive, what patterns are unique to their eye, what constitutes their aesthetic personality; the model builds a personalised layer on top of the frozen baseline that captures that individual variation. Every rating the user provides teaches the model something: a 1 teaches it what the user rejects; a 5 teaches it what captivates them; a 3, the uncertain middle, teaches it where their preferences are ambiguous and the model learns to navigate that ambiguity. The result is a predictor that understands both the broad consensus and the user's specific deviation from it, and it uses both together to anticipate who will catch that user's eye before they have even seen them. No other platform does this; every other system runs one model for all users, treating attraction as a population-level phenomenon with no room for individual taste; this system treats every user as a unique case requiring a unique model that adapts to them and to them alone. This explanation must be written as the centrepiece of the Phase 1 methodology section: the most compelling argument on the page for why the calibration step is not bureaucratic onboarding but a genuinely novel act of personalisation. The section background is `var(--cream)` with the same subtle maroon-light wash (`rgba(114, 47, 55, 0.04)`) that distinguishes the science card surfaces. It transitions into Section 4 via a background shift to `var(--blush)`.

**Section 4: Phase 2, Resonance.** A single centred column at `max-width: 900px`, because Resonance is the most intimately argued section, the one that must slow the user down and earn the trust of the personality methodology. The Venn diagram coalescing animation sits centred in this column, mid-section: the prose appears above it, the animation fires as the prose is being read (on scroll-entry to the animation element), and the shared-trait text appears in the overlap zone as the circles converge, then the prose continues below the Venn describing the three-heart match confirmation system and the personalised insights report. The read-more expandable at the section base reveals the astrology effect framing and the body of evidence demonstrating that perceived similarity predicts initial attraction far more powerfully than actual trait similarity, a finding replicated across hundreds of studies and multiple decades of research. Scroll-triggered fade-ins fire on the section title first, then on the prose paragraphs in sequence, then on the Venn animation container (which fires the coalescing animation on its own scroll-entry), then on the lower prose. The section background is `var(--blush)`.

**Section 5: Phase 3, Chemistry.** A layout that mirrors Phase 1's two-column approach, but with sides swapped: the visual element (the DNA-derived Chemistry phase icon in its animated state, larger than the card-size version, at approximately 160px square, centred in the left column) leads, and the prose with the read-more expandable follows in the right column. This inversion creates rhythm across the Phase 1/Phase 3 pair and prevents the three phase sections from feeling like repetitions of the same template. The section carries a note-box at its base, using the existing `note-box` component precisely, gold left border, gold gradient background, framing the HLA research as a body of evidence accumulated over decades: the note-box tells the story of why the science matters, not where it was published; it uses no paper titles, no author names, no parenthetical year citations; the register is declarative and accessible, building the case through accumulated description of what the research has consistently demonstrated, which is that people tend to be attracted to partners with different HLA profiles, that this preference appears to operate below the level of conscious awareness, and that it may carry biological significance for immune diversity in offspring; the argument is the body of evidence, not the bibliography. The read-more expandable covers HLA genetics research, the 200 DNA kit allocation for London, and the critical-mass expansion model for non-London users. The section background returns to `var(--cream)` with a subtle radial gold ambient glow at the center (matching the section's higher register of scientific ambition), transitioning into Section 6 via padding.

**Section 6: What You Get.** A centred section at `max-width: 800px` using a different structural template from the phase cards: a vertical list of three or four benefit items, each formatted as a small heading in DM Sans font-weight 600 navy at `1.1rem` followed by one to two sentences of supporting text in slate at `1rem`, the items separated by `1px solid rgba(212, 168, 83, 0.15)` horizontal rules and carrying a small gold icon or roundel to their left. The items are: your visual preference profile, your personality radar chart, your personalised match insights report, and your tripartite compatibility report (Phase 3 only). The section title in Cormorant Garamond appears first, fade-in; then the items fade in one by one with stagger delays; then the section's repeated email capture row at the base. The background is `var(--blush)`.

**Section 7: Tell Your Friends.** A compact section, narrower than the others at `max-width: 600px` centred, containing the share card described in Section 2 and nothing else. It requires no additional visual element because its simplicity is the point: the ask is direct, the interface is uncluttered, the copy-link action is immediate. The section sits between the What You Get section and the final CTA on a `var(--cream)` background, flush-padded above and below at `4rem`, bookended by thin gold section dividers (`border-top: 1px solid rgba(212, 168, 83, 0.15)`). Scroll-triggered fade-in on the entire card as a unit.

**Section 8: CTA and Footer.** A final section using the `var(--blush)` background, centred single column, carrying a short Cormorant Garamond heading at `2rem`, one sentence of supporting text in DM Sans slate, and the email capture row repeated from the hero. Below the email capture, a small DM Sans line in slate at `0.9rem` with a link to `contact.html` for direct enquiries. Below this section sits the standard footer in maroon-deep, identical in structure to every other page on the site: "Harmonia Engine" in Cormorant Garamond gold-champagne, the four footer links, and the copyright line. No modification to the footer structure. Scroll-triggered fade-in fires on the section heading, then on the body text, then on the email capture row.

## Section 4: SVG Icon Concepts

### Phase 1: Calibration Icon

The icon speaks to the act of assessment, the eye that has learned to see through data: a horizontal axis line, thin gold at `stroke-width: 1.5`, crossing the `100x100` viewBox from left to right at the midpoint `y=50`, with a short perpendicular tick mark at the center `x=50`. Above and below this axis, five evenly spaced vertical tick marks at `x=20, 35, 50, 65, 80` in gold at `stroke-width: 1`, each at different heights to suggest a measurement distribution, the tallest at `x=50` (the center, the median, the calibration point). A maroon dot sits at the top of the center tick, the calibration point fixed. The animation sequence: on card-entry, the horizontal axis draws itself left to right via `stroke-dashoffset` from `80` to `0` over `0.8s ease-out`; then the five tick marks draw upward from the axis one by one in left-to-right sequence, each via its own `stroke-dashoffset` transition at `0.15s` per tick with `0.1s` stagger; finally the maroon dot at center pops in at `scale(0)` to `scale(1)` via spring cubic-bezier over `0.25s`. The settled icon is a calibration graph: spare, precise, and measurable. The gold drop-shadow (`filter: drop-shadow(0 0 8px rgba(212, 168, 83, 0.25))`) is applied to the icon container as it reaches the header position, identical to how the existing card icons settle.

### Phase 2: Resonance Icon

The icon for Resonance extends the Venn circle vocabulary into the card-icon scale: two overlapping circles at approximately `r=28`, centers at `(36, 50)` and `(64, 50)`, in `stroke: var(--gold)` at `stroke-width: 1.5`, no fill, with a maroon dot at each circle's outer edge (leftmost point of left circle at `(8, 50)`, rightmost of right circle at `(92, 50)`) and a gold dot at the center of the overlap zone `(50, 50)`. The animation sequence: the two circles draw themselves simultaneously via `stroke-dasharray` and `stroke-dashoffset`, one tracing clockwise and the other counter-clockwise, over `1s ease-out`; then the maroon outer dots appear at `opacity: 0` to `1` via `0.2s ease`; then the overlap zone fills with a gold circle at `r=10`, center `(50, 50)`, at `fill: rgba(212, 168, 83, 0.2)` and `stroke: var(--gold)` at `stroke-width: 0.5`, scaling from `0` to `1` via the same spring cubic-bezier used throughout the existing animation system; and finally the inner gold dot at `r=3` pops in at the center. The settled icon is two circles recognising each other: the structural echo of the larger Venn coalescing animation in the Phase 2 section body, reduced to the 100px card scale.

### Phase 3: Chemistry Icon

The Chemistry icon is a simplified, card-scale derivative of the DNA helix that already exists in the why-harmonia genetic card, but with the third signal integrated: a double helix drawn in gold and maroon, but with a third horizontal connector added to the visible rungs, the connector rendered not as a solid line but as a dashed gold stroke at `stroke-dasharray: 3 2`, to signify the third signal's presence alongside the first two. The animation sequence follows the existing DNA helix vocabulary: the helix paths draw themselves via `stroke-dashoffset` from `200` to `0` over `1.1s ease-out`, then the rungs appear at `opacity: 0.4` to `1` with a gold drop-shadow glow pulse at `0.3s ease`, then the dashed third connectors draw themselves in at `stroke-dashoffset` from `15` to `0` over `0.4s ease`, a final beat that signals the addition of the genetic layer to the two-strand visual. The maroon accent appears as a dot at the midpoint of each solid rung, `r=2.5`, popping in at the moment the rungs reach full opacity. The icon settles with the same `filter: drop-shadow(0 0 8px rgba(212, 168, 83, 0.25))` used across all card icons.

### Static Venn Diagram Illustration (Phase 1, Calibration Section)

The static Venn is a concept illustration, not an icon: rendered at approximately `280px x 180px` SVG, centred in the right column of the Phase 1 section. Two circles at `r=75`, centers at `(85, 90)` and `(195, 90)`, overlapping at approximately `110` units of horizontal separation (less than the diameter, creating a clear overlap zone). Left circle stroke: `var(--maroon)` at `stroke-width: 1.5`, fill `rgba(114, 47, 55, 0.08)`. Right circle stroke: `var(--gold)` at `stroke-width: 1.5`, fill `rgba(212, 168, 83, 0.08)`. In the overlap zone, a `clipPath` intersection fill of `rgba(212, 168, 83, 0.15)`. Left circle label in Cormorant Garamond at `0.9rem` slate: "You". Right circle label: "Them". Center label at `0.7rem` DM Sans in maroon, centred in the overlap: "Shared traits". Below the diagram at `0.75rem` DM Sans slate in italics: "Perceived similarity, measured across seven dimensions." No animation. No hover state. A diagram, serving the argument of the text beside it.

### Coalescing Venn Animation (Phase 2, Resonance Section)

As specified in Section 2, this is a `240x180` SVG with two circles of `r=72`, initially positioned at `cx=60` and `cx=180` (separated, no overlap), transitioning on scroll-entry via CSS class addition to `cx=100` and `cx=140` (60-unit overlap). The circles are both in `fill: rgba(114, 47, 55, 0.3)`, `stroke: var(--gold)`, `stroke-width: 1.5`. The transition uses `transition: cx 1.2s cubic-bezier(0.4, 0, 0.2, 1)` applied via SVG attribute animation (or alternatively via CSS `transform: translateX()` on group elements wrapping each circle, since CSS transitions on SVG `cx` attributes are not universally supported). A third SVG group, clipped to the intersection path using a `clipPath` element computed at the resolved circle positions, carries the overlap zone fill at `rgba(212, 168, 83, 0.15)` and a centered `text` element in DM Sans at `0.8rem` maroon, initial `opacity: 0`, transitioning to `opacity: 1` over `0.4s ease` after a `0.9s` delay (as the circles complete their approach). Below the SVG, at `0.75rem` DM Sans slate: "Shared personality traits appear in the space between you." This copy fades in at `delay-4`. On mobile, the SVG scales to `max-width: 100%` and the two circles are scaled proportionally.

### 1-to-5 Rating Scale Visual (Phase 1, Calibration Section)

A horizontal SVG at `260px x 60px`, carrying five numbered nodes connected by a thin gold path. Each node is a circle at `r=16`, in `fill: var(--card-bg)`, `stroke: var(--gold)` at `stroke-width: 1`. The numeral inside each circle is in Cormorant Garamond at `0.9rem` maroon. The connecting path between nodes is a straight horizontal gold line at `stroke-width: 1`, `rgba(212, 168, 83, 0.4)`. Below each node, a label in DM Sans at `0.6rem` slate, uppercase, `letter-spacing: 0.05em`: "Not my type" below node 1, "Uncertain" below node 3, "Very attractive" below node 5. Nodes 2 and 4 have no label, only their numeral. The node at position 3 (the center) carries a slightly larger `r=18` to suggest the neutral center-point of the scale, with no other visual distinction. This is a static element; no animation; it is a communication tool, not a demonstration.
